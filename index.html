FAQs

What does the address check show? +
The check provides a comprehensive AML risk score from 0% to 100%, where 0% means the wallet is completely clean and 100% means it is highly suspicious. You'll see a breakdown across 7 key metrics: transaction diversity, wallet activity, CEX interactions, average transaction value, wallet age, suspicious transaction patterns, and interactions with high-risk addresses. Each metric is color-coded: green (low risk), amber (medium), red (high risk).
What do the parameters in the check results mean? +
Transaction diversity — how varied the wallet's interactions are. Low diversity can indicate a wallet used exclusively for one type of activity.

Wallet activity — overall transaction frequency and volume.

CEX transactions — interactions with known centralized exchanges. High CEX usage is generally positive.

Suspicious transactions — failed or flagged transactions that match known fraud patterns.

High-risk addresses — how many transactions involved wallets linked to scams, hacks, or sanctions.
How do I understand risk assessment? +
Our risk score works on a simple scale:

• 0–20% — Clean. No suspicious activity detected. Safe to transact.
• 21–45% — Low Risk. Minor flags, likely false positives. Proceed with awareness.
• 46–70% — Medium Risk. Notable suspicious patterns. Investigate before transacting.
• 71–100% — High Risk. Strongly associated with fraud, scams, or sanctions. Avoid interaction.

The score is calculated using GoPlus Security's real-time database and on-chain analytics.
How quickly is the balance replenished? +
Your payment is confirmed on-chain within seconds on the Solana network, which has an average block time of ~400ms. Once your transaction is confirmed (typically 1–3 seconds), the report generation begins immediately. The full AML scan and report delivery takes under 30 seconds from payment confirmation. You will receive your report in the same session — no email required.
What does the percentage risk score mean? +
The percentage represents the average risk across all 7 evaluated metrics. A score of 12% means the wallet has an overall very low association with risky activity. Think of it like a credit score in reverse — the lower the score, the cleaner the wallet. Scores above 50% warrant caution. Scores above 70% strongly suggest the address is associated with illicit activity such as phishing, money laundering, or sanctioned entities.
How does AML Check help to protect you against blocking? +
Exchanges and financial institutions use AML software to screen incoming transactions. If a wallet you've received funds from is flagged, your account can be frozen without warning. By running an AML check before accepting funds or interacting with an unknown wallet, you verify that the source is clean. This documentation can also be used as evidence when appealing an exchange's compliance department if your account is incorrectly flagged.
The risk is higher than 50%, but I'm certain the address is reliable. What to do? +
A score above 50% doesn't necessarily mean the wallet owner is a criminal — it may reflect past exposure to questionable counterparties or mixers without the owner's knowledge. If you trust the address, we recommend: (1) contacting our team for a deeper manual review, (2) asking the wallet owner to provide proof of funds origin, and (3) documenting the transaction thoroughly. Our AML specialists can provide a written assessment that you can share with exchanges if needed.
What is the difference between an address and transaction checks? +
Address check — analyzes the overall history and risk profile of a wallet address. Useful for evaluating a counterparty before any interaction.

Transaction check — analyzes a specific transaction hash to trace the flow of funds and verify the origin and destination of that particular transfer.

We recommend running an address check before engaging with a new wallet, and a transaction check when receiving large or unexpected incoming transfers.
How often are checks recommended to do? +
We recommend running a check: (1) Before any large transaction with a new or unknown wallet, (2) When receiving significant inbound funds from an unfamiliar source, (3) Quarterly for wallets you regularly interact with, as their risk profile can change over time, (4) Before listing on exchanges to ensure your wallet won't be flagged during compliance screening. Risk profiles change as new wallet activity is indexed — a wallet that was clean 6 months ago may not be clean today.
What cryptocurrencies does AML Check analyze? +
Currently AML Check focuses on the Solana ecosystem, covering all SOL, SPL tokens (including USDC, USDT, BONK, JUP, and others), and Solana-based DeFi interactions. We cross-reference against 25+ risk categories including exchange hacks, darknet markets, ransomware wallets, phishing addresses, mixers/tumblers, and OFAC sanctioned entities. Support for Ethereum, BNB Chain, and Polygon is coming soon.
What if I still need more checks? +
Each payment covers one wallet check. If you need to check multiple wallets, simply run the process again for each address. For high-volume needs (10+ checks per month), contact us on Telegram for a bulk pricing arrangement. For businesses or exchanges that need API access to run automated checks, we offer an enterprise plan with dedicated support and volume discounts.
Is my payment information stored? +
No personal data is stored. The only record is the on-chain transaction hash, which is publicly visible on the Solana blockchain like any other transaction. We do not store your wallet address, your report results, or any identifying information. Your payment goes directly on-chain to the service wallet — there is no intermediary holding your funds. The entire process is permissionless and non-custodial.
